{% trans ' What is Air-Gaging ?' %}

{% blocktrans %} Air gaging, also known as pneumatic gaging or air gauging, is a metrological technique used for precision measurements in manufacturing and quality control processes. It involves the use of compressed air to measure dimensional characteristics of a workpiece. The basic principle of air gaging is to use the interaction between a stream of pressurized air and the surface of the workpiece to make accurate measurements. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans 'Reason for Air-gaging ?' %}

{% trans 'Air gaging is commonly used in situations where high precision is required, especially in industries like machining, automotive manufacturing, and aerospace. It is known for its speed, accuracy, and the ability to measure tight tolerances. Air gaging is particularly suitable for measuring features like bores, grooves, and other internal dimensions where traditional contact methods might be challenging or impractical.' %}
